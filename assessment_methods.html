4. Assessment Methods Selection Module

Selecting appropriate assessment methods is crucial for gathering meaningful data about student achievement of learning outcomes. This module guides you through choosing and implementing effective assessment strategies for each of your program learning outcomes.

Step 1: Understanding Assessment Approaches

Direct vs. Indirect Assessment

Direct Assessment Methods:
  • Examine actual student work or performances
  • Provide evidence of what students can do or produce
  • Examples: exams, papers, projects, presentations, portfolios, performances
Indirect Assessment Methods:
  • Gather perceptions, opinions, or reflections about learning
  • Provide contextual information about the learning experience
  • Examples: surveys, interviews, focus groups, exit interviews, employer feedback

Formative vs. Summative Assessment

Formative Assessment:
  • Occurs during the learning process
  • Provides feedback to improve learning
  • Low-stakes, developmental in nature
  • Examples: drafts, practice exercises, classroom assessment techniques
Summative Assessment:
  • Occurs at the conclusion of learning
  • Evaluates achievement of outcomes
  • Higher-stakes, evaluative in nature
  • Examples: final exams, capstone projects, comprehensive portfolios

Step 3: Developing Assessment Tools

Rubric Development

Rubrics provide clear criteria and standards for evaluating student work. Effective rubrics:

  • Define specific criteria aligned with learning outcomes
  • Describe performance levels for each criterion
  • Use clear, descriptive language
  • Maintain consistent scale across criteria

Assessment Prompts and Instructions

Clear prompts and instructions ensure students understand what they need to demonstrate:

Elements of Effective Assessment Prompts:
  • Clearly state the task or question
  • Connect explicitly to learning outcomes
  • Specify format, length, and other requirements
  • Provide evaluation criteria or rubric
  • Include submission instructions and deadlines

Example Assessment Prompt (Critical Analysis):

Critical Analysis Assignment

Learning Outcome Addressed: Analyze theoretical perspectives in the discipline using appropriate evidence and reasoning.

Task: Select one of the theoretical frameworks discussed in class (Framework A, B, or C) and analyze its strengths and limitations when applied to the case study provided.

Requirements:

  • 1500-2000 words, double-spaced
  • Include at least 5 scholarly sources
  • Address historical development, key concepts, and practical applications
  • Evaluate the framework's explanatory power using specific examples from the case
  • Propose at least one modification that would address an identified limitation

Evaluation: Your analysis will be evaluated using the Critical Analysis Rubric provided in the course materials, with particular attention to the quality of your reasoning, use of evidence, and depth of analysis.

Submission: Upload your paper as a Word document to the course management system by [date/time].

Step 4: Sampling Strategies

For programs with large enrollments, sampling can make assessment manageable:

Sampling Approaches:

  • Random sampling: Select a percentage of student work randomly
  • Stratified sampling: Ensure representation across relevant categories (e.g., concentrations)
  • Longitudinal sampling: Track the same students over time
  • Cross-sectional sampling: Assess students at different program stages simultaneously

Sample Size Guidelines:

  • For small programs (<50 students), assess all students
  • For medium programs (50-100 students), sample 40-50%
  • For large programs (>100 students), sample 25-30% or at least 30 students

Step 5: Creating an Assessment Schedule

Develop a multi-year assessment plan that distributes the assessment workload:

Assessment Cycle Considerations:

  • Assess each outcome at least once within the program review cycle
  • Prioritize outcomes based on program needs and external requirements
  • Balance assessment activities across academic years
  • Allow time for implementing improvements before reassessment

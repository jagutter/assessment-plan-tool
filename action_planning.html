6. Action Planning Module

The action planning process transforms assessment findings into meaningful improvements to your program. This module guides you through developing and implementing action plans based on assessment results to enhance student learning and program effectiveness.

Step 1: Reviewing Assessment Findings

Begin by synthesizing key findings from your assessment activities:

Learning Outcome Key Findings Strengths Identified Areas for Improvement Supporting Evidence

Example Assessment Summary (Communication Program):

Learning Outcome Key Findings Strengths Identified Areas for Improvement Supporting Evidence
Deliver effective oral presentations 82% of students met or exceeded expectations for organization and delivery; 45% met expectations for responding to questions Students effectively organize and deliver prepared content Students struggle with impromptu responses to audience questions Capstone presentation rubric scores; faculty observation notes
Produce well-written documents for diverse audiences 75% of students met expectations for professional audiences; 40% met expectations for general audiences Students write effectively for disciplinary audiences Students struggle to adapt content for non-specialist audiences Portfolio assessment scores; employer feedback

Step 2: Prioritizing Areas for Improvement

Not all findings require immediate action. Use these criteria to prioritize:

Improvement Area Impact on Student Learning Alignment with Program Goals Feasibility Resource Requirements Priority Level

Prioritization Questions:

  • How significant is the gap between expected and actual performance?
  • How central is this outcome to the program's mission and goals?
  • How many students are affected by this issue?
  • Are there external requirements (accreditation, etc.) related to this area?
  • What resources would be required to address this issue?
  • Is this an emerging issue or a persistent problem?

Step 3: Developing Action Plans

For each priority area, develop a specific action plan:

Action Plan 1

Step 1

Example Action Plan (Communication Program):

Improvement Area: Students struggle to adapt written communication for non-specialist audiences

Desired Outcome: Students will demonstrate the ability to effectively communicate complex disciplinary concepts to general audiences

Action Steps:

  1. Revise assignments in COM 301 to include more writing for non-specialist audiences

    • Person(s) responsible: Dr. Smith, course coordinator
    • Timeline: Revise by August 15, implement in Fall semester
    • Resources needed: Sample assignments, 10 hours of development time
    • Success indicators: Revised syllabus and assignment descriptions

  2. Develop a rubric specifically for evaluating communication to non-specialist audiences

    • Person(s) responsible: Assessment committee
    • Timeline: September 1-30
    • Resources needed: Committee meeting time, example rubrics
    • Success indicators: Completed rubric approved by faculty

  3. Conduct a workshop for faculty on teaching adaptation strategies for different audiences

    • Person(s) responsible: Dr. Johnson, faculty development coordinator
    • Timeline: October 15
    • Resources needed: $500 for materials and refreshments, 2-hour time block
    • Success indicators: Workshop attendance, faculty feedback

Evaluation Plan:

  • Collect samples of student writing for non-specialist audiences from COM 301 and capstone course
  • Assess using new rubric at the end of Spring semester
  • Compare results to baseline data from previous assessment
  • Target: 70% of students will meet expectations for non-specialist audience writing

Step 4: Implementing Action Plans

Implementation Checklist:

Common Implementation Challenges and Solutions:

Challenge Potential Solutions
Faculty resistance to change Involve faculty in planning; emphasize benefits; provide support and resources
Limited resources Prioritize actions; seek external funding; implement changes incrementally
Time constraints Integrate with existing activities; distribute workload; extend timeline if necessary
Coordination across courses Establish regular communication; create shared resources; designate a coordinator
Maintaining momentum Set interim milestones; celebrate progress; regularly revisit the plan

Step 5: Documenting and Communicating Improvements

Documentation Framework:

  1. Initial Assessment Findings: Summarize what prompted the action plan
  2. Action Plan Implementation: Document what was actually done (vs. what was planned)
  3. Follow-up Assessment: Present results from reassessment after changes
  4. Impact Analysis: Analyze the effectiveness of the changes
  5. Lessons Learned: Reflect on the improvement process
  6. Next Steps: Outline future plans based on current results

Communication Strategies:

  • Program Website: Post summaries of improvements and their impact
  • Annual Reports: Include assessment-based improvements in program reports
  • Faculty Meetings: Discuss results and celebrate successes
  • Student Communication: Inform students about changes made based on assessment
  • Stakeholder Updates: Share relevant improvements with external stakeholders
  • Institutional Assessment Office: Submit documentation for institutional records

Example Documentation (Communication Program):

Initial Assessment Findings (Spring 2024):

Assessment of student portfolios revealed that while 75% of students could effectively communicate to professional audiences, only 40% met expectations for adapting content for general audiences.

Action Plan Implementation (Fall 2024):
  1. Revised COM 301 to include three new assignments focused on communicating to non-specialist audiences
  2. Developed and implemented a new rubric specifically for evaluating audience adaptation
  3. Conducted faculty workshop on teaching communication adaptation strategies (15 faculty attended)
Follow-up Assessment (Spring 2025):

Reassessment of student portfolios showed 68% of students now meet expectations for communicating to general audiences, an increase of 28 percentage points.

Impact Analysis:

The curriculum revisions and faculty development activities have substantially improved students' ability to adapt their communication for different audiences. The target of 70% was nearly achieved in one academic year.

Lessons Learned:
  1. Faculty benefited from concrete examples of effective audience adaptation
  2. The specialized rubric helped clarify expectations for students
  3. Multiple practice opportunities throughout the curriculum were essential
Next Steps:
  1. Extend similar assignments to COM 201 to introduce concepts earlier
  2. Develop additional resources for faculty and students
  3. Continue monitoring performance with a new target of 80%

3. Curriculum Mapping Module

Curriculum mapping is a visual representation of how your program's courses contribute to student achievement of learning outcomes. This module guides you through creating a comprehensive curriculum map that identifies where outcomes are introduced, reinforced, and mastered across your program.

Step 1: Understanding Curriculum Mapping

Purpose of Curriculum Mapping

Curriculum mapping serves several important functions:

  1. Ensures comprehensive coverage of all learning outcomes across the curriculum
  2. Identifies gaps or redundancies in outcome coverage
  3. Shows progression of student learning throughout the program
  4. Facilitates communication among faculty about curriculum design
  5. Guides assessment planning by identifying key points for data collection
  6. Supports curriculum review by visualizing the relationship between courses and outcomes

Curriculum Map Components

A complete curriculum map includes:

  • Program learning outcomes listed as column headers
  • Required courses listed as row headers (typically in sequence)
  • Indicators showing the relationship between each course and outcome
  • Legend explaining the meaning of indicators used in the map

Step 2: Selecting Mapping Indicators

  • ✓ = The course addresses this outcome
  • blank = The course does not address this outcome
  • I = Introduced: Students are introduced to the knowledge, skill, or value
  • R = Reinforced: Students develop and practice the knowledge, skill, or value
  • M = Mastered: Students demonstrate mastery of the knowledge, skill, or value
  • A = The course includes formal assessment of this outcome
  • F = The course includes formative assessment of this outcome
  • S = The course includes summative assessment of this outcome
  • P = Primary emphasis: The outcome is a primary focus of the course
  • S = Secondary emphasis: The outcome is addressed but not a primary focus
  • M = Minimal emphasis: The outcome is touched upon briefly

Step 4: Analyzing Your Curriculum Map

Use these questions to analyze your completed curriculum map:

Coverage Analysis:
  • Are all program learning outcomes addressed in multiple courses?
  • Are there any outcomes that receive limited attention in the curriculum?
  • Are there courses that don't significantly contribute to any program outcomes?
Sequence Analysis:
  • Is there a logical progression (I→R→M) for each outcome throughout the curriculum?
  • Are outcomes introduced early enough to allow for development?
  • Are there appropriate opportunities for mastery before graduation?
Assessment Analysis:
  • Are there designated points for assessing each outcome?
  • Is assessment distributed appropriately throughout the curriculum?
  • Are there both formative and summative assessment opportunities?
Balance Analysis:
  • Is there a balanced emphasis across all program outcomes?
  • Are core disciplinary competencies adequately emphasized?
  • Are there any courses that are overloaded with too many outcomes?

Step 5: Refining Your Curriculum

Based on your analysis, consider these potential curriculum refinements:

Addressing Gaps:
  • Modify existing courses to incorporate underrepresented outcomes
  • Develop new courses to address specific outcomes
  • Create co-curricular experiences to supplement course-based learning
Improving Progression:
  • Resequence courses to create more logical development
  • Adjust prerequisites to ensure proper skill building
  • Modify course content to better support outcome development
Enhancing Assessment:
  • Designate specific courses for outcome assessment
  • Develop common assignments or rubrics across courses
  • Create capstone experiences that integrate multiple outcomes

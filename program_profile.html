1. Program Profile Module

The Program Profile is the foundation of your assessment plan. This module helps you define your program's identity, purpose, and goals, establishing the context for all subsequent assessment activities.

Step 1: Program Information

Basic Information

Provide a brief overview of your program (100-200 words)

Step 2: Program Mission Statement

Mission Statement Guidelines:

  • Clearly state the program's purpose
  • Identify the primary functions and activities
  • Reflect the distinctive character of the program
  • Align with institutional mission and values
  • Be concise (typically 3-5 sentences)

Mission Statement Template:

The mission of the [Program Name] is to [primary purpose/function], by [primary activities/methods], in order to [intended outcomes/impact]. We value [core values] and are committed to [distinctive commitments].

Example Mission Statements

+
Biology (Bachelor's):

The mission of the Biology program is to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of biological principles and processes across scales from molecules to ecosystems. Through rigorous coursework, laboratory experiences, and research opportunities, we prepare students for careers in biological sciences, healthcare, and related fields. We value scientific inquiry, environmental stewardship, and inclusive excellence in biological education and research.

Educational Leadership (Master's):

The Educational Leadership program prepares transformative leaders who advance educational equity and excellence in diverse contexts. Through collaborative learning experiences, field-based projects, and critical reflection, our students develop the knowledge, skills, and dispositions to lead positive change in educational organizations. We are committed to social justice, evidence-based practice, and the development of learning communities that support all students' success.

Step 3: Program Goals

Goal Development Guidelines:

  • Focus on program-level aspirations rather than specific student learning
  • Align with program mission and institutional priorities
  • Address key stakeholder needs (students, employers, community)
  • Consider both educational and operational aspects of the program
  • Limit to 4-6 goals for manageability

Example Program Goals

+
Computer Science (Bachelor's):
  1. Prepare students for successful careers in computing and technology fields
  2. Develop students' ability to solve complex problems using computational thinking
  3. Foster innovation and entrepreneurship in computing applications
  4. Build a diverse and inclusive community of computer scientists
  5. Maintain currency with rapidly evolving technologies and industry practices
Public Health (Master's):
  1. Prepare public health professionals who can address complex health challenges
  2. Advance health equity through evidence-based practice and policy
  3. Foster interdisciplinary collaboration to improve population health
  4. Engage communities as partners in public health initiatives
  5. Contribute to the public health knowledge base through research and scholarship

Step 4: Stakeholder Identification

Primary Stakeholders:

  • Students: Current and prospective students
  • Faculty: Program faculty and instructors
  • Alumni: Graduates of the program
  • Employers: Organizations that hire program graduates
  • Graduate Programs: For undergraduate programs feeding into graduate education
  • Accreditors: Program or specialized accreditation bodies
  • Community Partners: Organizations that collaborate with the program
